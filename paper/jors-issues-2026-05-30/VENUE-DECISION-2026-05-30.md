# MIRROR venue decision, 2026-05-30

Selected working route: **Journal of Open Research Software (JORS), Issues in Research Software**.

Status: public-release route. No external venue submission, signature, timestamp, or editor contact was performed in this gate.

## Why this venue

MIRROR is strongest as an experience/report article about creating and maintaining reusable research-software evidence packages. It is not yet best presented as a pure software metapaper because the central claim is methodological and because there is not yet independent reuse evidence. JORS Issues in Research Software is a better fit because it explicitly accepts experience reports, position papers, surveys, and articles on research-software ecosystems and practice.

## Requirements checked

Source: JORS Submission Guidelines, checked 2026-05-30.

- Issues in Research Software covers creating, maintaining, and evaluating open source research software.
- Accepted formats include experience reports, position papers, survey papers, education/training articles, ecosystem articles, and impact-measurement articles.
- Suggested length is 3000-4000 words, roughly 4-6 pages including figures and references.
- The section asks for an abstract, no more than three heading levels, encouraged display items, and Vancouver numbered references.
- There is no Issues template; submit PDF plus source file, DOC, or ODT.
- JORS asks for source URLs where available, permissions/licensing statements, a cover letter, and reviewer suggestions.

## Sample papers used as style anchors

- Fehr et al. 2021, *Sustainable Research Software Hand-Over*, JORS 9(1):5.
- Lamprecht et al. 2022, *What Do We (Not) Know About Research Software Engineering?*, JORS 10(1):10.
- Katz et al. 2019, *The State of Sustainable Research Software*, JORS 7(1):11.
- Barker et al. 2025, *Institutional Policy Pathways for Supporting Research Software*, JORS 13:39.

Observed style: compact title page, direct abstract, numbered sections, display items used for synthesis rather than decoration, explicit back matter, Vancouver references with DOI/URL.

## Rejected routes

- **JOSS**: plausible for a shorter companion software paper if rewritten into the JOSS Markdown structure and centered on installable, reusable MIRROR software. It is weaker for the current essay because the present contribution is methodological rather than only software availability.
- **Software Impacts**: plausible for a short companion impact article after the release DOI, because the journal expects compact software impact papers. It would need a sharper impact-overview paragraph and less thesis-style argument.
- **Data & Policy**: topically adjacent, but the vault already has active Data & Policy traffic nearby; MIRROR would also need a stronger policy audience rewrite.
- **DTRAP / security venues**: possible later if MIRROR is reframed around artifact-evaluation security or signed handoff, but the current paper is research-software practice rather than threat research.
